\noindent\textbf{Abstract.} I replicate the finite-horizon experimental
asset market of \citet{dufwenberg2005bubbles} inside a deterministic
browser-based simulator and extend it with the expected-utility
messaging population of \citet{lopezlira2025llm}. I then introduce a
new paradigm---\emph{Wang~2026}---in which each Lopez-Lira utility
agent's starting subjective valuation is drawn, once per run, from a
live ChatGPT call whose prompt encodes the agent's risk preference,
bias mode, and the presence of experienced counterparties. The returned
psychological anchor~$\hat{V}_i$ is clamped to
$[0.25\,\FV_0,\,1.75\,\FV_0]$, seeds the agent's prior on its first
decision tick, and then yields to the standard Lopez-Lira
belief-blending channel. The research question is whether the asset
ultimately ends up in the hands of the agent with the highest anchored
valuation. The testbed reports per-tick matching, a normalised
valuation gap, Haessel~$R^2$, normalised deviation, amplitude, turnover
and allocative efficiency; the merged Experienced-plus-Utility
population is split as $\lceil (N{-}N_{\text{fixed}})/2\rceil$
experienced and the remainder utility. The simulator preserves
reproducibility through a seeded mulberry32 PRNG and a deterministic
fallback when the language-model call fails, so every finding can be
replayed without network access. This paper documents the replication,
the new anchor channel, and the market-quality instruments used to
answer the allocation question.

\medskip
\noindent\textbf{Keywords:} experimental asset markets, bubbles,
expected utility, large language models, ChatGPT, allocative
efficiency.
