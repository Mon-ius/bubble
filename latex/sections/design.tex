\section{Experimental Design}
\label{sec:design}

\subsection{Three paradigms}

The simulator implements three paradigms on a shared market substrate.

\emph{Strict-DLM.}---Exactly six DLM traders ($N{=}6$), three type-A
(200\textcent, 6 shares) and three type-B (600\textcent, 2 shares),
with identical buy-and-hold value of 1000\textcent.  Endowment types
are assigned by Fisher-Yates shuffle.  No utility agents, no
messaging.

\emph{Lopez-Lira.}---A mix of fundamentalists (F), trend followers
(T), a zero-intelligence agent (R), and utility agents (U).  Cash is
drawn from $\text{Uniform}[800,1200]$, inventory from
$\text{Uniform}\{2,3,4\}$.  Utility agents carry risk preferences
apportioned by a user-specified split
($\alpha_L{+}\alpha_N{+}\alpha_A{=}100$).  Belief update follows
Plan~I.

\emph{AIPE (AI-Agent Prior Elicitation).}---Same population as
Lopez-Lira, but the belief update switches to Plan~II or Plan~III.
Before trading begins, a one-shot LLM call seeds each utility agent's
initial valuation (clamped to $[25,175]$~cents).

\subsection{Session structure}

Following DLM, a session is four consecutive ten-period rounds played
by the same population.  At each round boundary the engine:
(i)~records round-final cash for payoff accounting,
(ii)~increments every agent's $k_i$,
(iii)~in strict-DLM mode at the round-3 boundary, executes the
round-4 replacement protocol,
(iv)~rewinds cash and inventory to initial endowments,
(v)~clears the order book, and
(vi)~resets the period counter.
Trust matrices, experience counters, and agent identity persist
across rounds.  Session payoff is
$\pi_i = \sum_{r=1}^{4}\text{finalCash}_{r}[i] + 500$.

\subsection{Round-4 replacement}

At the round-3$\to$round-4 transition in strict-DLM mode, the engine
selects $k\in\{2,4\}$ veterans uniformly at random and replaces them
with fresh traders ($k_i{=}0$, iid type-A/B endowment).  Following
DLM's own notation:
\begin{itemize}[nosep]
\item \textbf{T2} (R4-$\sfrac{2}{3}$): two replacements, four
  veterans---two-thirds experienced;
\item \textbf{T4} (R4-$\sfrac{1}{3}$): four replacements, two
  veterans---one-third experienced.
\end{itemize}
A batch runner executes ten sessions (five T2, five T4, interleaved)
and tabulates per-session bubble statistics.

\subsection{Identification}

All randomness flows through a single seeded mulberry32 PRNG.  No
agent code calls the system random-number generator.  For any
(population, seed) pair, Plan~I produces identical output across
runs.  Plans~II and~III introduce one exogenous source of
variation---the LLM reply---handled by the silent degradation to
Plan~I on failure.  Comparisons across plans hold the seed fixed, so
any difference in market statistics is attributable to the belief
channel.  The sample RNG is derived from the engine seed by an XOR
offset, so endowment edits do not alter the trading sequence for
unedited agents.
