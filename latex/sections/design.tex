\section{Experimental Design}
\label{sec:design}

\subsection{The asset and the fundamental}

The asset is a finite-lived claim on an iid per-period dividend
that pays either zero or twenty experimental cents with equal
probability. Letting $d$ denote the period-$t$ dividend draw,
\begin{equation}
d\sim\text{Uniform}\{0,20\},\qquad \E[d]=10,\qquad T=10.
\label{eq:dividend}
\end{equation}
The asset lives for $T=10$ periods and pays nothing thereafter,
so its risk-neutral fundamental value at the start of period~$t$ is
\begin{equation}
\FV_t \;=\; \E[d]\cdot(T-t+1) \;=\; 10\cdot(T-t+1),
\label{eq:fv}
\end{equation}
that is, a linearly declining staircase from $\FV_1=100$ to
$\FV_T=10$ with a terminal value of zero after the final dividend
pays. Equation~\eqref{eq:dividend} and equation~\eqref{eq:fv}
reproduce the paper constants of~\citet{dufwenberg2005bubbles}
exactly; both the number of periods and the dividend mean are
surfaced read-only in the simulator's ``Paper constants'' panel and
are not user~tunable, so that every comparison across populations
holds the market structure fixed.

\subsection{Continuous double auction with period discretisation}

The simulator discretises each period into $K=18$ decision rounds,
so a run comprises $T\cdot K=180$ ticks. Every tick, each agent
posts at most one order, routed to a price--time-priority order
book; a crossing pair matches at the resting price. At the end of
each period, outstanding quotes are withdrawn and the dividend is
drawn from~\eqref{eq:dividend} and credited to each holder. The
$K=18$ discretisation is pinned in the ``Simulator constants''
panel and is not user~tunable.

\subsection{Sampling and endowments}

At $t=0$ the simulator draws a flat list of per-agent specifications
from the user's chosen population mix. For each slot, cash is
sampled uniformly from $[800,1200]$ experimental cents and initial
inventory is drawn uniformly from $\{2,3,4\}$ shares, so that total
endowments are heterogeneous but bounded. Every slot is also
assigned a randomly drawn personal name (from an internal name
list) and a type label such as F1, U3, E2, which is used in the
agents panel and the trust matrix for human legibility. Crucially,
the sampling RNG is derived from $\text{seed}\oplus\mathtt{0xA5A5A5A5}$
and is independent of the engine RNG; this is the
invariant that lets a user edit cash or inventory on an existing
draw and re-run without re-sampling agent types, while still
producing the same per-tick trading sequence as a matched run
without the~edit.

\subsection{Populations}

Four canonical population presets are available. Table~\ref{tab:presets}
summarises their composition and the qualitative outcome each is
intended to showcase.

\begin{table}[h]
\centering
\small
\caption{Population presets}
\label{tab:presets}
\begin{tabular}{l l l}
\toprule
Preset & Composition & Expected outcome \\
\midrule
Inexperienced    & 2\,T\,+\,2\,R\,+\,1\,F\,+\,1\,E               & Classic bubble and crash       \\
Experienced      & 3\,E\,+\,2\,F\,+\,1\,T                        & Tight convergence to $\FV_t$   \\
Mixed            & 2\,F\,+\,2\,E\,+\,1\,T\,+\,1\,R               & Closest tracking of $\FV_t$    \\
Utility          & 6\,U                                          & Lopez-Lira default             \\
\bottomrule
\end{tabular}
\end{table}

The ``Number of Agents'' slider rescales the E and U slots
proportionally while pinning the fixed F/T/R background at
$N_{\text{fixed}}=4$. The paradigm switch in the navbar overrides
these presets: clicking DLM~2005 fills the user-controllable slots
with experienced agents ($E=N-N_{\text{fixed}}$, $U=0$); clicking
Lopez-Lira~2025 fills them with utility agents ($E=0$,
$U=N-N_{\text{fixed}}$); clicking AIPE splits the slots as
$\lceil(N-N_{\text{fixed}})/2\rceil\to E$ and the remainder to $U$, so
that both subject types share one market and the extended panels
stay populated. The rounding is asymmetric on purpose: at
capacity~$c=1,3,5$ the E~block wins the odd slot, which
preserves the bubble-suppressing channel in small populations
where it matters~most.

\subsection{Reproducibility}

Every run is driven by a single scalar seed. A mulberry32 generator
$s_{k+1}=(s_k+\mathtt{0x6D2B79F5})\bmod 2^{32}$ produces the
$(0,1)$-uniform stream consumed by every stochastic step---dividend
realisations, zero-intelligence quotes, the noise term in the
utility prior, and the trust-update ordering. Calls to
\texttt{Math.random} from agent or market code are a contract
violation that breaks reproducibility by the (population, seed)
pair and are flagged as an invariant in the project style guide.
The seed is re-rolled only on Reset, and the replay system
reconstructs past states by slicing append-only history arrays on
\texttt{Market} and \texttt{Logger}---no frame is ever recomputed.
