\section{Experimental Design}
\label{sec:design}

\subsection{Session structure: four consecutive rounds}

The unit of study in~\citet{dufwenberg2005bubbles} is a
\emph{session} of four consecutive ten-period markets played by the
same six traders. The paper insists on the terminology explicitly:
\begin{quote}
``A session involved four consecutive markets. In the following, we
shall talk in terms of four different rounds. Note the distinction
between rounds and periods; a round (being a market) consists of
ten periods.''~\citep[p.~1733]{dufwenberg2005bubbles}
\end{quote}
The session wrapper is the load-bearing element of the original
design: bubbles inflate in round~1, attenuate in rounds~2--3, and
have effectively died by round~4 even though the population is
identical---experience, not composition, kills the bubble. The
simulator therefore wraps the per-round market in a session loop of
$R=4$ rounds. At every round boundary the order book is cleared,
each agent's cash and inventory are rewound to its sampled spec,
and the period counter resets to one. Learned state---trust
matrices, belief modes, the EU candidate cache---is intentionally
preserved across the boundary so that the cross-round learning
channel that drives the paper's headline result is the only source
of persistent change between consecutive markets. Both
$R$ and $T$ are surfaced read-only in the simulator's ``Paper
constants'' panel and are not user tunable.

\subsection{The asset and the fundamental}

The asset is a finite-lived claim on an iid per-period dividend
that pays either zero or twenty experimental cents with equal
probability. Letting $d$ denote the period-$t$ dividend draw,
\begin{equation}
d\sim\text{Uniform}\{0,20\},\qquad \E[d]=10,\qquad T=10.
\label{eq:dividend}
\end{equation}
The asset lives for $T=10$ periods within each round and pays
nothing thereafter, so its risk-neutral fundamental value at the
start of period~$t$ of any round is
\begin{equation}
\FV_t \;=\; \E[d]\cdot(T-t+1) \;=\; 10\cdot(T-t+1),
\label{eq:fv}
\end{equation}
that is, a linearly declining staircase from $\FV_1=100$ to
$\FV_T=10$ with a terminal value of zero after the final dividend
pays. The fundamental resets to $\FV_1=100$ at the start of every
new round, so the price chart traces a four-tooth saw-tooth across
the full session. Equation~\eqref{eq:dividend} and
equation~\eqref{eq:fv} reproduce the dividend process and the
fundamental schedule of~\citet{dufwenberg2005bubbles} exactly. The
session length $R$, the per-round asset life $T$, and the dividend
mean $\E[d]$ are all surfaced read-only in the simulator's ``Paper
constants'' panel so that every comparison across populations holds
the market structure fixed.

\subsection{Continuous double auction with period discretisation}

The simulator discretises each period into $K=18$ decision rounds,
so a single round comprises $T\cdot K=180$ ticks and a full
four-round session comprises $R\cdot T\cdot K=720$ ticks. Every
tick, each agent posts at most one order, routed to a price--time
priority order book; a crossing pair matches at the resting price.
At the end of each period the outstanding quotes are withdrawn and
the dividend is drawn from~\eqref{eq:dividend} and credited to each
holder. At the end of period~$T$ of every non-final round the
session loop also rewinds endowments, clears the book, and resets
the period counter to one before the next round begins. The $K=18$
discretisation is the simulator's own approximation of the
two-minute continuous z-Tree window used in the paper, is pinned in
the ``Simulator constants'' panel, and is not user tunable.

\subsection{Sampling and endowments}

At $t=0$ the simulator draws a flat list of per-agent specifications
from the user's chosen population mix. For each slot, cash is
sampled uniformly from $[800,1200]$ experimental cents and initial
inventory is drawn uniformly from $\{2,3,4\}$ shares, so that total
endowments are heterogeneous but bounded. This is a deliberate
relaxation of the two discrete endowment types in the original
study---half the traders started with 200~cents and six shares and
the other half with 600~cents and two shares so that the implied
buy-and-hold value was identical at $1{,}000$~cents---and trades a
sharper baseline against DLM for a smoother sweep over the
population mix when the user moves the agent-count slider. Every
slot is also assigned a randomly drawn personal name (from an
internal name list) and a type label such as F1, U3, E2, which is
used in the agents panel and the trust matrix for human
legibility. Crucially, the sampling RNG is derived from
$\text{seed}\oplus\mathtt{0xA5A5A5A5}$ and is independent of the
engine RNG; this is the invariant that lets a user edit cash or
inventory on an existing draw and re-run without re-sampling agent
types, while still producing the same per-tick trading sequence as
a matched run without the~edit. The same per-agent endowment is
also used by the round-end reset described above, so every round
of a session starts each trader from the same balance schedule even
though the within-round sequence of trades evolves freely.

\subsection{Populations}

Four canonical population presets are available. Table~\ref{tab:presets}
summarises their composition and the qualitative outcome each is
intended to showcase.

\begin{table}[h]
\centering
\small
\caption{Population presets}
\label{tab:presets}
\begin{tabular}{l l l}
\toprule
Preset & Composition & Expected outcome \\
\midrule
Inexperienced    & 2\,T\,+\,2\,R\,+\,1\,F\,+\,1\,E               & Classic bubble and crash       \\
Experienced      & 3\,E\,+\,2\,F\,+\,1\,T                        & Tight convergence to $\FV_t$   \\
Mixed            & 2\,F\,+\,2\,E\,+\,1\,T\,+\,1\,R               & Closest tracking of $\FV_t$    \\
Utility          & 6\,U                                          & Lopez-Lira default             \\
\bottomrule
\end{tabular}
\end{table}

The ``Number of Agents'' slider rescales the E and U slots
proportionally while pinning the fixed F/T/R background at
$N_{\text{fixed}}=4$. The paradigm switch in the navbar overrides
these presets: clicking DLM~2005 fills the user-controllable slots
with experienced agents ($E=N-N_{\text{fixed}}$, $U=0$); clicking
Lopez-Lira~2025 fills them with utility agents ($E=0$,
$U=N-N_{\text{fixed}}$); clicking AIPE splits the slots as
$\lceil(N-N_{\text{fixed}})/2\rceil\to E$ and the remainder to $U$, so
that both subject types share one market and the extended panels
stay populated. The rounding is asymmetric on purpose: at
capacity~$c=1,3,5$ the E~block wins the odd slot, which
preserves the bubble-suppressing channel in small populations
where it matters~most.

\subsection{Reproducibility}

Every run is driven by a single scalar seed. A mulberry32 generator
$s_{k+1}=(s_k+\mathtt{0x6D2B79F5})\bmod 2^{32}$ produces the
$(0,1)$-uniform stream consumed by every stochastic step---dividend
realisations, zero-intelligence quotes, the noise term in the
utility prior, and the trust-update ordering. Calls to
\texttt{Math.random} from agent or market code are a contract
violation that breaks reproducibility by the (population, seed)
pair and are flagged as an invariant in the project style guide.
The seed is re-rolled only on Reset, and the replay system
reconstructs past states by slicing append-only history arrays on
\texttt{Market} and \texttt{Logger}---no frame is ever recomputed.
A single seed therefore reproduces the entire 720-tick session
end-to-end, rounds and round-end resets included.
