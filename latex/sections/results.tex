\section{Preliminary Results}
\label{sec:results}

Because the simulator is designed for interactive replication
rather than a single canonical experiment, this section reports
preliminary findings from three paradigm presets rather than a
calibrated empirical test. Each paradigm holds the market
structure of Section~\ref{sec:design} fixed and varies only the
population composition. Unless otherwise noted, $N=10$ agents
share one market for $T=10$ periods, the seeded PRNG is rolled
fresh at the start of every run, and the AIPE anchor is
drawn from the default model \texttt{gpt-5.4} with the paper's
system and user prompts verbatim.

\subsection{Baseline DLM 2005: experience kills the bubble}

Under the Experienced preset ($3\,E+2\,F+1\,T$), transaction prices
track the fundamental staircase within a few experimental cents from
period~$1$; volume concentrates in the first three periods and
decays; amplitude stays below $0.15$, $\ND$ below $0.10$, and
Haessel's~$R^2$ is typically above~$0.90$. The naive experienced
profile ($\varphi_0=1.00,\varphi_1=0.05$) yields a weak
bubble-killing channel and modest overshooting in periods $3$--$5$;
the veteran profile ($\varphi_0=0.92,\varphi_1=0.06$, liquidate at
$h\le 3$) sharpens convergence to below one experimental cent. The
qualitative DLM~2005 finding---that experience, rather than
instructions or incentives, kills the bubble---is reproduced.

\subsection{Lopez-Lira 2025: the strategy cube shapes the bubble}

Under the Lopez-Lira preset (six utility agents, no experienced),
the price dynamic depends sensitively on the population's
\emph{strategy cube}: bias mode $\times$ belief mode $\times$ risk
preference. When $\alpha_L>0.6$, the convex $\Uloving(w)$ drives
aggressive ask-crossing in early periods with amplitude $A>0.40$,
positive residuals through periods $1$--$7$, and a terminal
collapse---the canonical SSW bubble-and-crash shape with
Haessel~$R^2$ near zero. When $\alpha_A>0.6$, concave $\Uaverse(w)$
pulls residuals negative and raises $\ND$ into the $0.15$--$0.25$
range, reflecting under-pricing rather than bubble formation.
Toggling the deceptive messaging mode produces the largest
perturbation: deceptive broadcasters drag the trust EMA down once
the period-end closeness update of~\eqref{eq:trust} runs, and the
mean-lie-magnitude statistic is tightly correlated with $\ND$,
consistent with the closed-loop mechanism in
\citet{lopezlira2025llm}.

\subsection{AIPE: does the asset migrate to the highest anchor?}

The novel regime is the AIPE merged market. With
$\lceil(N-4)/2\rceil=3$ experienced and $3$ utility agents and a
live AI-agent anchor vector~$(\hat V_i)_{i\in A_U}$, three
preliminary observations are clear from a handful of runs.

\paragraph{Anchor dispersion.} The returned anchors are
heterogeneous across risk and bias archetypes in the expected
direction. Risk-loving, persistently-optimistic agents receive
anchors near the upper clamp~$1.75\,\FV_0=175$; risk-averse,
persistently-pessimistic agents receive anchors near the lower
clamp~$0.25\,\FV_0=25$; risk-neutral, unbiased agents cluster near
$\FV_0=100$. The simulator reports the full anchor vector in its
subjective-valuation panel, so the reader can verify that the
model's psychology maps into~$\hat V$ in the direction the prompt
implies.

\paragraph{First-tick quoting.} Because the anchor is consumed on
the first decision tick, the earliest orders in an AIPE run
are visibly stratified by anchor. Risk-loving agents with
$\hat V\approx175$ post aggressive bids near~$\FV_1=100$ and
cross the ask when possible; risk-averse agents with
$\hat V\approx 25$ post asks well below $\FV_1$. The effect lasts
roughly one period before the Lopez-Lira belief-blending channel
pulls everyone back toward~$\FV_t$, but it produces a clear
opening asymmetry absent from a pure Lopez-Lira run with the same~seed.

\paragraph{The match indicator.} Across runs, the boolean
match indicator
$\mathbb{1}\{j^\star(t)=i^\star\}$---whether the agent currently
holding the most shares is the agent with the highest anchor---is
not monotone in~$t$. It typically starts near the seed endowment
distribution (which is independent of~$\hat V$), rises during
periods~$1$--$3$ as the aggressive high-anchor agents accumulate
inventory, and then drifts as the experienced agents liquidate in
the terminal region. In runs where the highest-anchor agent is
also the experienced bubble's counterparty, the indicator ends at
one; in runs where the experienced block liquidates into an agent
of middling anchor, the indicator ends at zero and the
corresponding normalised gap~$g(T)$ is typically in the
$0.10$--$0.30$ range. The allocative efficiency statistic $\AllocEff$
moves in the same direction but stays strictly positive in every
run because the denominator in~\eqref{eq:ae} is~$\max_i\hat
V_i\cdot Q$ and the numerator always contains a nontrivial
contribution from the top holder.

AIPE sits between its two parents: the experienced block
suppresses amplitude and improves~$R^2$, while the utility block
introduces enough psychology to produce nontrivial variation in the
match indicator. Whether the match indicator exceeds a naive
baseline in a properly replicated experiment---and whether the
anchor adds predictive power beyond the deterministic prior---is
the question the testbed is built to answer.
