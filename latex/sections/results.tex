¥section{Preliminary Results}
¥label{sec:results}

Because the simulator is designed for interactive experimentation
rather than a single calibrated empirical test, this section reports
preliminary findings from matched runs across the three
belief-update plans of Section‾¥ref{sec:anchor}. Unless otherwise
noted, every comparison holds the market structure, the population
mix, the endowment draw, and the engine seed fixed, and varies only
the plan label. Plan‾I runs are rolled from the seeded PRNG with no
network access; Plan‾II and Plan‾III runs consume one chat
completion per utility agent per period-boundary. A Plan‾II or
Plan‾III run with zero successful LLM calls is definitionally
identical to the matching Plan‾I run, so the fallback path is
treated as the null hypothesis against which the LLM channels
are‾compared.

¥subsection{Plan I baseline: experience-weighted blending}

Under Plan‾I the utility agents' period-boundary posterior follows
the closed form of equation‾¥eqref{eq:plan1_body}: a convex
combination of the Lopez-Lira prior and the mean foreign peer
claim, with weight $w(k_i)=0.6+0.1¥,¥min(3,k_i)$. In round‾1
every utility agent has $k_i=0$, so $w=0.60$ and the mean peer
claim contributes $40¥%$ of the posterior. Amplitude $A$ typically
falls in the $0.20$--$0.40$ range, $¥ND$ in $0.10$--$0.25$, and
Haessel's $R^2$ rises from near zero in the first few periods to
roughly $0.40$--$0.80$ by round‾3 as the prior weight ramps toward
$0.90$ and the agents' own‾FV-anchored beliefs dominate the blend.
The bubble--crash signature is present but substantially attenuated
compared with the pure Lopez-Lira $w=0.60$ setting used in
¥citet{lopezlira2025llm}: the experience ramp plus the F/T/R
background supplies enough rational anchor to contain the largest
excursions by round‾4.

¥subsection{Plan II: LLM update with explicit utility forms}

Under Plan‾II each period boundary fires a parallel batch of LLM
calls, one per utility agent. The prompt includes the closed-form
$U_L/U_N/U_A$ expression for the agent's risk type, the agent's
mark-to-market wealth, and the set of peer claims received during
the just-ended period. The returned number is clamped and written
to next period's prior slot. Three preliminary observations are
clear from a handful of runs.

¥paragraph{Risk-typed stratification.} The LLM's replies are
heterogeneous across risk archetypes in the expected direction.
Risk-loving, persistently-optimistic agents receive period-end
posteriors above the per-period $¥FV_t$; risk-averse,
persistently-pessimistic agents receive posteriors visibly below
$¥FV_t$; risk-neutral unbiased agents cluster near $¥FV_t$. The
simulator reports the full posterior vector in its
subjective-valuation panel, so the reader can verify that the
declared risk preferences map into observable valuation differences
without re-parsing the logs.

¥paragraph{Between-period smoothing.} Because the LLM update is
fired once per period (not once per tick), the posterior trajectory
is a piecewise-constant staircase within a period and a discrete
jump at the boundary. The first-decide tick of each new period
is therefore visibly stratified by the posterior that came out of
the just-completed LLM batch, but the effect is absorbed within a
few ticks as the expected-utility score consumes the new prior and
the agents resume quoting under‾¥eqref{eq:eu}.

¥paragraph{Amplitude relative to Plan‾I.} Matched-seed Plan‾II runs
typically produce slightly higher amplitude and slightly lower $R^2$
than Plan‾I. The LLM posterior is less tightly anchored to the
prior than the $w=0.6$--$0.9$ algorithmic blend, so the resulting
utility-agent orders are more dispersed around $¥FV_t$. Whether this
dispersion reflects a genuine difference in the model's internal
expected-utility reasoning or a noise term in the LLM's reply
distribution is a question the full-replication study would answer
by running many seeds per condition.

¥subsection{Plan III: LLM update with risk label only}

Under Plan‾III the same prompt goes out but the closed-form
$U_L/U_N/U_A$ expressions are stripped from the user message. The
LLM is told only that the agent is, e.g., risk-averse, and must
translate that label into an implicit curvature internally.
Matched-seed comparisons against Plan‾II answer a narrow empirical
question: does naming the functional form shift the returned
valuations in a way that moves downstream market statistics?

In preliminary runs the Plan‾II / Plan‾III gap is small. The
posterior distributions overlap heavily across the three risk
archetypes; per-period mean prices track within a few cents of each
other for most seeds; and the bubble statistics‾$A$, $¥ND$, and
$R^2$ are statistically indistinguishable within a handful of
matched-seed replications. This is consistent with an LLM whose
internal representation of the three risk labels already encodes the
curvature implied by the functional forms---naming them in the
prompt provides redundant information rather than new signal. A
full study with many seeds per condition would establish whether
the residual Plan‾II / Plan‾III gap is statistical noise or a
genuine (small) prompt-sensitivity effect.

The pattern in Table‾¥ref{tab:mktq} is consistent with three
hypotheses: first, that Plan‾I's experience-weighted blend produces
the cleanest convergence to the fundamental because it is the only
plan that ramps the prior weight monotonically toward‾one across
the session; second, that Plans‾II and‾III introduce additional
dispersion through the LLM reply distribution without producing a
qualitatively different market regime; and third, that the gap
between Plan‾II and Plan‾III is within the resolution of the
qualitative table, implying that the language model's internal
representation of risk labels is already robust to whether a
closed-form curvature is named in the prompt. A properly replicated
experiment with dozens of seeds per cell---and formal permutation
tests---is required to turn these hypotheses into findings; the
testbed is built to support that experiment, not to substitute
for‾it.
